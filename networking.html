Networking & Connections

Why Networking Matters

Networking is more than asking for jobs—it's about building genuine relationships, learning from others, and sharing your story. Most opportunities emerge through people. The UMSI community and resources below will help you get started and stay consistent.

Building a Strategic Networking Plan

Treat networking like a project: define goals, list target roles/organizations, and create a weekly routine for outreach and follow-ups. Start small (1-2 messages per week) and increase as you gain confidence.

Steps to Get Started

  • Set a clear weekly outreach goal (e.g., 2 emails).
  • Track your progress in a spreadsheet or notebook.
  • Review your conversations monthly and adjust your goals.

Outreach Messages

Keep outreach short, respectful, and specific about what you hope to learn. Offer a brief 20–25 minute chat and be flexible with time.

Planning an Informational Interview

An informational interview is a short conversation to learn about a person's role, team, and path. It's not about asking for a job—focus on insights and relationship-building.

  1. Research their background (role, projects, recent posts).
  2. Draft 4-6 questions using the TIARA lenses (Trends, Insights, Advice, Resources, Assignments).
  3. Wrap up on time, thank them, and send a brief follow-up note with a key takeaway.
  • TIARA Method (question examples and structure)

Career Fair Preparation

Fairs are condensed networking: clarify goals, research employers, prepare your 30-second pitch, and plan follow-ups the same day.

  • Draft a brief introduction that connects your skills to the organization’s work.
  • Skim each employer’s recent news and open roles; note 1–2 questions per table.
  • After the event, send thank-you notes and add contacts to your tracker.
