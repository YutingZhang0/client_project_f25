Career Exploration

Career exploration is a continuous process of discovering your interests, strengths, and values—and then aligning them with opportunities in the job market. At UMSI, you have access to curated tools, alumni insights, and practical guides that make it easier to clarify your direction. Whether you’re experimenting with new ideas, preparing for interviews, or considering part-time roles, the resources below will help you take informed steps forward.

Using AI Effectively in Career Preparation

Artificial intelligence is becoming a valuable tool for career development. With well-crafted prompts, you can generate draft resumes, cover letters, or interview practice questions. However, it’s important to approach AI as a partner, not a replacement—your critical thinking, editing, and authenticity are still essential. The AI Prompt Resource Guide provides concrete examples of prompts you can adapt for your own career journey, from brainstorming resume bullet points to preparing for behavioral interviews.

Use them to gain perspective and generate questions for your own informational interviews. Planning Your Job and Internship Search

Searching for jobs or internships is most effective when you treat it as a structured project. Instead of sporadically browsing postings, plan your search like a class assignment: set milestones, keep a spreadsheet of applications, and track deadlines. This approach reduces stress, helps you notice patterns in job descriptions, and ensures you don't miss opportunities. UMSI provides a planning spreadsheet to help you organize target roles, companies, and timelines—making the process more intentional and less overwhelming.

Exploring Part-Time Roles During School

Part-time work can provide both financial support and career-relevant experience while you study. Many UMSI students find roles on campus, in Ann Arbor, or through remote opportunities. These positions help you strengthen transferable skills such as communication, teamwork, and time management. The key is to balance work commitments with your academic responsibilities and professional goals. The CDO maintains resources to help you evaluate whether part-time work is right for you and to find postings that match your skills and schedule.
